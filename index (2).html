The Six Points of CROCUS

A Historically Reformed Soteriology

What is CROCUS?

The Six Points of CROCUS present a historically Reformed, monergistic soteriological framework. It is similar to TULIP but introduces several important distinctions. While TULIP remains a widely used summary of Reformed soteriology, its terminology is often revised even by its own proponents due to potential misunderstandings. CROCUS seeks to express the historic Reformed view of salvation with greater theological precision and a practical, pastoral emphasis. It also offers a more faithful reflection of the Canons of Dort regarding the atonement and accommodates both high and moderate views.

The Six Points of CROCUS

Letter Doctrine Summary
C Comprehensive Depravity All human faculties are corrupted, and we are unable to believe apart from grace.
R Ruin to Restoration Adam's fall ruined us; Christ restores us to God.
O Outworked Salvation God's grace sustains us to the end — sanctification to glory.
C Conquering Grace God effectually calls and regenerates His elect.
U Unconditional Election God’s choice is not based on foreseen faith.
S Secured Redemption Christ's work guarantees the elect’s salvation by God’s effectual application.

Why the Crocus?

The crocus flower has long been considered a symbol of hope, joy, and new life. It is one of the first flowers to bloom every year and often pushes up through the snow, announcing the end of winter and the beginning of spring.
