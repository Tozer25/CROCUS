What is CROCUS?

The Six Points of CROCUS present a historically Reformed, monergistic soteriological framework. It is similar to TULIP but introduces several important distinctions...

The Six Points of CROCUS

Letter Doctrine Summary
CComprehensive DepravityAll human faculties are corrupted, and we are unable to believe apart from grace.
RRuined to Restored RelationshipAdam's fall ruined us; Christ restores us to God.
OOutworked SalvationGod's grace sustains us to the end — sanctification to glory.
CConquering GraceGod effectually calls and regenerates His elect.
UUnconditional ElectionGod’s choice is not based on foreseen faith.
SSecured RedemptionChrist's work guarantees the elect’s salvation by God’s effectual application.

Why the Crocus?

The crocus flower has long been considered a symbol of hope, joy, and new life. It is one of the first flowers to bloom every year and often pushes up through the snow, announcing the end of winter and the beginning of spring. It is a wonderful illustration of spiritual rebirth and the new life believers have in Christ.
