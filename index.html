What is CROCUS?

The Six Points of CROCUS present a historically Reformed, monergistic soteriological framework. It is similar to TULIP but introduces several important distinctions. While TULIP remains a widely used summary of Reformed soteriology, its terminology is often revised even by its own proponents due to potential misunderstandings. CROCUS seeks to express the historic Reformed view of salvation with greater theological precision and a practical, pastoral emphasis. It also offers a more faithful reflection of the Canons of Dort regarding the atonement and accommodates both high and moderate views. In doing so, it honors the rich history and diversity of the Reformed tradition.

The Six Points of CROCUS

Letter Doctrine Summary
C Comprehensive Depravity All human faculties are corrupted by sin, and we are utterly unable to believe, repent, or seek God without the prior work of His grace.
R Ruin to Restoration Adam’s fall brought ruin to all humanity, but Christ brings true restoration and reconciliation through His redemptive work.
O Outworked Salvation Salvation is not only initiated by God’s grace but also sustained and brought to completion by His ongoing work — from regeneration to glorification.
C Conquering Grace God effectually calls His elect and irresistibly brings them to new life in Christ through the power of the Holy Spirit.
U Unconditional Election God’s choice of those who will be saved is not based on foreseen faith or merit but solely on His sovereign will and grace.
S Secured Redemption Christ’s atoning work infallibly secures the salvation of the elect, ensuring that all for whom He died will be brought to faith and kept by God’s power.

Why the Crocus?

The crocus flower has long been considered a symbol of hope, joy, and new life. It is one of the first flowers to bloom every year and often pushes up through the snow, announcing the end of winter and the beginning of spring. It is a wonderful illustration of spiritual rebirth and the new life believers have in Christ.
